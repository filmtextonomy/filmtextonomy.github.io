What Is a Film Genre?

A film genre is a category that defines a movie by its narrative conventions, tone, and audience expectations. Genres help viewers anticipate certain themes, characters, or styles—such as horror, comedy, or melodrama. This article explores the significance of genre in both Western and non-Western cinematic traditions.

Iranian scholar Alireza Kaveh suggests distinguishing genre from related concepts such as style, format, and tradition—especially in cross-cultural cinema.
