Christine Gledhill and Feminist Genre Theory

Christine Gledhill is a key figure in feminist film theory and its intersection with genre studies. She argues that genres are not fixed structures but sites of ideological negotiation.

Her work emphasizes how gender roles are encoded and contested through genre conventions, especially in melodrama and soap operas.

Gledhill challenges structuralist models by highlighting the dynamic interplay between audience interpretation, cultural context, and genre formation.
